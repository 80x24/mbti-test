# MBTI Question Mapping Guide

16personalities.com uses 60 questions on a 7-point scale: -3 (Strongly Agree) to 3 (Strongly Disagree).

## Dimensions

| Dimension | -3 (Agree) direction | +3 (Disagree) direction |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Energy | Introverted (I) | Extraverted (E) |
| Mind | Intuitive (N) | Observant (S) |
| Nature | Feeling (F) | Thinking (T) |
| Tactics | Judging (J) | Prospecting (P) |
| Identity | Turbulent (T) | Assertive (A) |

## Question Categories

### Energy (I/E) — Questions about social interaction, solitude preference

**Pro-Introvert (Agree = I):**
- "networking or promoting yourself is daunting"
- "enjoy solitary hobbies more than group ones"
- "wait for others to introduce themselves"
- "avoid making phone calls"
- "would love a job working alone"

**Pro-Extravert (Agree = E, so Disagree = I):**
- "regularly make new friends"
- "comfortable walking up to someone interesting"
- "enjoy participating in team-based activities"
- "prefer to be around others"
- "friends describe you as lively and outgoing"
- "can easily connect with people you just met"
- "drawn to busy, bustling atmospheres"

### Mind (N/S) — Questions about abstract thinking, theory, imagination

**Pro-Intuitive (Agree = N):**
- "complex and novel ideas excite you"
- "enjoy experimenting with new approaches"
- "actively seek out new knowledge areas"
- "enjoy debating ethical dilemmas"
- "drawn to creative expression"
- "enjoy exploring unfamiliar ideas"
- "prefer creative solutions over concrete steps"

**Pro-Observant (Agree = S, so Disagree = N):**
- "not interested in interpretations of creative works"
- "cannot imagine writing fictional stories"
- "bored when discussion gets highly theoretical"
- "not interested in discussing theories about the future"
- "pondering abstract philosophy is a waste of time"

### Nature (T/F) — Questions about logic vs emotion in decisions

**Pro-Thinking (Agree = T):**
- "prioritize facts over people's feelings"
- "favor efficiency even if disregarding emotions"
- "prioritize proving your point over preserving feelings"
- "not easily swayed by emotional arguments"
- "base choices on objective facts"

**Pro-Feeling (Agree = F, so Disagree = T):**
- "more persuaded by emotional resonance than facts"
- "people's stories speak louder than numbers"
- "prioritize being sensitive over honest"
- "when facts and feelings conflict, follow your heart"
- "emotions control you more than you control them"
- "focus more on how people might feel"
- "rely on emotional intuition than logical reasoning"
- "act on decisions that feel right without proof"

### Tactics (J/P) — Questions about planning, organization, structure

**Pro-Judging (Agree = J):**
- "living and working spaces are clean and organized"
- "prioritize and plan tasks, completing before deadline"
- "like to use organizing tools like schedules and lists"
- "prefer to do chores before relaxing"
- "like to have a to-do list for each day"
- "plans interrupted → priority is getting back on track"
- "complete things methodically without skipping steps"

**Pro-Prospecting (Agree = P, so Disagree = J):**
- "often allow the day to unfold without schedule"
- "often do things at the last possible moment"
- "challenging to maintain consistent schedule"
- "work style is spontaneous bursts of energy"
- "struggle with deadlines"

### Identity (T/A) — Questions about self-confidence, worry, emotional stability

**Pro-Turbulent (Agree = T):**
- "small mistake causes doubt in overall abilities"
- "prone to worrying things will take a turn for worse"
- "mood can change very quickly"
- "still bothered by mistakes from long time ago"
- "when someone thinks highly of you, wonder when they'll be disappointed"
- "often feel overwhelmed"

**Pro-Assertive (Agree = A, so Disagree = T):**
- "usually stay calm under pressure"
- "rarely worry about making good impression"
- "rarely second-guess choices"
- "rarely feel insecure"
- "feel confident things will work out"

## Answer Mapping Strategy

For each character, determine where they fall on each dimension, then:

1. **Strong signal questions** (core to the dimension): Use -3 or 3
2. **Moderate signal questions**: Use -2 or 2
3. **Weak/ambiguous questions**: Use -1, 0, or 1

**Watch for negatively-worded questions** — e.g. "You believe pondering philosophy is a WASTE of time." An intuitive character should DISAGREE (positive value), not agree.

### Example Mapping (INTJ-T character)

For a dry, introverted, analytical, philosophical character:
- Energy: Strong I → all social questions get 2-3 (disagree), solitude questions get -2 to -3 (agree)
- Mind: Strong N → theory/creativity questions get -2 to -3, "not interested in X" questions get 2-3
- Nature: Strong T → logic questions get -2 to -3, emotion questions get 2-3
- Tactics: Strong J → organization questions get -2, chaos questions get 2-3
- Identity: Moderate T → self-doubt questions get -1 to -2, confidence questions get 0-1
